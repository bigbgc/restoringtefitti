Oceans Affected by Pollution

Southern Ocean (Antarctic)

Comparatively cleaner, but climate change is melting ice, releasing stored pollutants.
Microplastics are now being found in Antarctic waters.
Heavy impact on penguins, krill, seals.

Arctic Ocean

Fastest warming ocean on Earth.
Melting ice releases trapped plastics & chemicals.
Oil drilling & shipping routes bring pollution.
Marine animals (polar bears, seals, whales) highly affected by toxins.

Atlantic Ocean

Oil spills (Gulf of Mexico, West Africa).
Plastic pollution from coastal cities (US East Coast, Europe, West Africa).
Coral reef damage in the Caribbean Sea due to warming & pollution.
High shipping traffic → ballast water introduces invasive species.

Pacific Ocean

Great Pacific Garbage Patch: The world’s largest collection of floating plastic between Hawaii & California.
High microplastics concentration → harming fish, turtles, seabirds.
Industrial waste & nuclear waste dumping (historically from Japan, US).
Coral reef bleaching in areas like Great Barrier Reef (Australia).

Indian Ocean

Plastic waste hotspot (due to poor waste management in South Asia & East Africa).
Oil pollution from Middle East shipping lanes.
Coral bleaching in Maldives, Seychelles, Lakshadweep.
High mercury levels due to industrial waste.
